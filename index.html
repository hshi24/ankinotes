It needs to make sense to you
No matter how hard you make the cards, if the cards doesn’t make sense to you, it will take forever to remember them. —> Bad cards!!
Important rules in creating cards
1. You need to CARE
Don’t add cards to Anki just to add cards
pointless facts is a waste of your time
What to do?
Picture yourself in settings where knowing an answer to your card would actually matter to you.
In what setting would this knowledge be of any use to you?
2. The cards needs to make sense to YOU
Remembering =/= Understanding
Make sure you understand what you’re trying to learn before you try to memorize it
If you don’t have a clue about the context, you won’t remember
no use remembering the card
What to do?
Study the material first before you convert it into Anki cards
By studying and thoroughly understanding what you’re learning you’ll also acquire enough information on the subject to create good quality cards
 
3. Less is more
Common beginner’s pitfall: cram too much information into one flashcard
The goal is not to put everything you need to learn into one small block of text
A good quality card is a simple card
otherwise why don’t you buy a notebook instead?
Make sure each card asks for one piece of information.
Learning is context sensitive, so make sure you know your card’s context
Make sure the cards is answerable
ask for simple and short answer that should never be longer than a few words
4. Involving your senses
Tie and connect to existing piece of knowledge
what to do?
add images to everything
mnemonic
hooks and connections
